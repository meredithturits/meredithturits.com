About Meredith

Meredith Turits lives and writes in Park Slope, Brooklyn—and doesnâ€™t yet own a stroller.

She is a magna cum laude graduate of Tufts University, where she studied Philosophy of Language and Linguistics. She earned a minor in Communications and Media Studies through Tufts and the School of the Museum of Fine Arts in Boston. Meredith is currently the senior culture editor at Bustle.com, where she directs all books content and works on special projects. She is one of the site's four founding editors. Meredith is also the fiction editor for the Brooklyn Quarterly.

Previously, Meredith served as the online associate editor at Glamour magazine, where she oversaw lifestyle content, and Get Inspired, the inspiring women/current events vertical. She spearheaded its launch in early 2012, and was lead writer and top editor of its anchor blog, The Conversation, which introduced content including politics, authors, current events, career, and influential women to the site's daily editorial lineup.

Her writing has also appeared in publications including The New Republic, BlackBook,The Rumpus, Full Stop, Bookslut, the Tottenville Review, Glamour, Gothamist, F'd in Park Slope, Performer, and Skyscraper. Short fiction has appeared in Joyland, Vol. 1 Brooklyn, Corium, The Squawk Back, Split Lip, Anobium, and Podium Literary Journal, and five by five hundred. Meredith blogs at Such Small Hands, and Tweets at @meredithturits.
